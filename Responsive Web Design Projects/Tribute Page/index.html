Michael Jordan's Life (thus far)

  • 1963: Born in Brooklyn, New York
  • 1981: Attended University of North Carolina Chapel Hill where he won an NCAA (National Collegaiet Atheletics Association) championship in 1982
  • 1984: Selected as the third overall pick by the Chicago Bulls in the 1984 NBA Draft
  • 1984: Nike gave Jordan his own signature shoe dubbed "Air Jordan." In the later years, the shoe brand would branch off into the Jordan Brand, which makes 1 billion dollars yearly for Nike
  • 1985: Won the NBA Rookie of the Year Award
  • 1988: Won the NBA Most Valuable Player Award (he would win again in 1991, 1992, 1996, and 1998)
  • 1991: Won his first NBA Finals, where he would end up winning five more times in 1992, 1993, 1996, 1997, and 1998. He also won the NBA Finals MVP awards in every Finals he played in.
  • 1992: Was a member of the U.S. National Basketball Team, dubbed "The Dream Team." The team would have an undefeated record in the 1992 Olympics, winning the gold medal in basketball.
  • 1993: Retired from basketball for one year to play baseball for the Birmingham Barons
  • 2003: Retired as a member of the Washington Wizards
  • 2006 - present: Chairman of the Charlotte Hornets
